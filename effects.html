What are the Effects?

Climate change leads to extreme weather, rising seas, and ecosystem disruptions. Higher temperatures affect agriculture and biodiversity. Global action to cut greenhouse gases is essential for a resilient and sustainable future.

Melting of Glaciers


This will create a plethora of problems for humankind and the animals living on the earth. Due to increased global warming, the level of the sea will rise which will lead to flooding. It will also endanger several species of animals and will hamper the balance of the ecosystem.

Hurricanes Frequency


As the temperature of the oceans rises, hurricanes and other storms are likely to become stronger. With the increase in global warming, the water in the ocean warms up and it heats up the surrounding air, creating hurricanes.

Unexpected Heat Waves


Because of greenhouse gases and other causes, unexpected streaks of severe weather are just the tips of the iceberg in global warming. Heat waves cause dangerously hot weather and in recent years, more deaths have occurred due to heat waves than in the last sixty years.Extreme heat events can trigger a variety of heat stress condition. It can also lead to water shortages and increased stress for plants.

Frequent Wildfires


While wildfires are a natural occurrence, with the added carbon dioxide in the air, and hotter summers, the evidence speaks for itself. The rate at which they burn is longer than the last, and with the release of carbon dioxide into the air, people as well as wildlife severely suffers. Each time a wildfire burns, the less oxygen there is to combat the dangerous amounts of carbon dioxide being released into the atmosphere.
